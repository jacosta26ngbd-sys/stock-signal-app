How signals are determined
BUY — Potential Opportunity
RSI below 32 (oversold) and/or Golden Cross detected (50-day MA above 200-day MA). Suggests the stock may be undervalued relative to recent momentum. Strongest when multiple indicators align.
SELL — Caution Advised
RSI above 68 (overbought) and/or Death Cross detected (50-day MA below 200-day MA). Suggests the stock may be overextended and due for a pullback. Not a guarantee — some stocks stay overbought for extended periods.
WATCH — Monitor Closely
RSI approaching extremes (30–40 or 60–70 range) but not yet there. Worth monitoring for a potential entry or exit developing. Set price alerts and check back frequently.
HOLD — Neutral Territory
RSI between 40–60, no strong MA crossover. No extreme signal present. Suitable to hold existing positions. Continue monitoring but no urgent action indicated.

RSI — Relative Strength Index. Measures momentum 0–100. Below 30 = oversold, above 70 = overbought.


Golden Cross — 50-day moving average crosses above 200-day. Classic long-term bullish signal.


Death Cross — 50-day moving average falls below 200-day. Classic long-term bearish signal.


52-Week Proximity — Stock within 15% of its yearly low may be in accumulation. Near yearly high may be overextended.

Always combine signals with fundamental research. Never act on a single indicator alone.
